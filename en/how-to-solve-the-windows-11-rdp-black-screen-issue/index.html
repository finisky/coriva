How to Solve the Windows 11 RDP Black Screen Issue

Step-by-step solutions to fix the black screen issue when using Remote Desktop Protocol on Windows 11.
Posted on 2024-12-14 ·  In Misc ·  2 min read ·   | Translations:
  • 中文

If you’ve encountered the dreaded black screen when using Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) on Windows 11, you’re not alone. This issue can be frustrating, especially if you rely on RDP for work or remote troubleshooting. This blog will guide you through the steps to fix this problem.

Why Does the Black Screen Issue Occur?#

The black screen problem often arises due to incompatibilities or bugs introduced in updated NIC drivers. These drivers are critical for managing your network connections, and any conflict can disrupt services like RDP. Rolling back to a previous version of the NIC driver can resolve these compatibility issues.

Step-by-Step Guide to Roll Back Your NIC Driver#

Step 1: Open Device Manager#

  1. Press Win + X or right-click on the Start button.
  2. Select Device Manager from the menu.

Step 2: Locate Your NIC#

  1. In the Device Manager window, expand the Network adapters category.
  2. Find your NIC (commonly named something like “Intel Ethernet” or “Realtek Network Controller”).

Step 3: Access Driver Properties#

  1. Right-click on your NIC and select Properties.
  2. Navigate to the Driver tab.

Step 4: Roll Back the Driver#

  1. Click the Roll Back Driver button. (If this option is greyed out, you may need to download a previous version of the driver from the manufacturer’s website.)
  2. Follow the on-screen instructions to complete the rollback.

Step 5: Restart Your Computer#

After the rollback, restart your computer to ensure the changes take effect.

Step 6: Test RDP#

  1. Open your RDP client and attempt to connect to the remote machine.
  2. Verify that the black screen issue is resolved.

Preventing Future Issues#

To avoid similar problems in the future:

  • Pause Driver Updates: Temporarily pause automatic driver updates using the Windows Update settings or a tool like Group Policy Editor.
  • Regular Backups: Always create a restore point or back up your drivers before updating them.
  • Stay Updated: Keep an eye on the manufacturer’s website for stable driver releases.

Conclusion#

The Windows 11 RDP black screen issue can be a significant hindrance, but rolling back your NIC driver is a quick and effective fix. By following the steps outlined above, you can restore your RDP functionality and get back to your tasks seamlessly.
